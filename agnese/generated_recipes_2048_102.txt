
=== Scenario 1, Recipe 1 ===

Query: I am allergic to nuts and tree nuts. Can you give me a Thai curry recipe free of nuts?

Recipe #1: "Spicy Shrimp and Pineapple Curry"

**Overview:**
Title: Spicy Shrimp and Pineapple Curry
Difficulty level: Medium
Time: 30 minutes (Prep: 15 minutes, Cook: 15 minutes, Total: 30 minutes)
Servings: 4
Comprehensive allergen and dietary compliance statement: This recipe is free from nuts and tree nuts, making it suitable for individuals with nut allergies. However, please note that this recipe contains shrimp, which may not be suitable for those with shellfish allergies.

**Ingredients:**

* 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
* 2 cups pineapple chunks
* 2 tablespoons coconut oil
* 1 onion, diced
* 2 cloves garlic, minced
* 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
* 1 teaspoon ground cumin
* 1 teaspoon curry powder
* 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
* 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
* 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
* 1 cup coconut milk
* Salt and pepper, to taste
* Fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish
* Allergen markings: (shrimp), (coconut oil), (coconut milk)

**Substitution options:**

* For shrimp, you can substitute with chicken breast or tofu for a vegetarian option.
* For pineapple, you can substitute with mango or papaya for a different flavor profile.

**Cross-contamination prevention notes:**

* Ensure that all utensils and cutting boards used for preparing the shrimp are thoroughly cleaned and sanitized to prevent cross-contamination with other allergens.
* If using a shared cooking space, ensure that all cooking surfaces and equipment are cleaned and sanitized before preparing the dish.

**Instructions:**

1. Heat the coconut oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
2. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger and cook until the onion is translucent, about 3-4 minutes.
3. Add the cumin, curry powder, turmeric, and cayenne pepper and cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly.
4. Add the diced tomatoes and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally.
5. Add the coconut milk and bring the mixture to a simmer.
6. Add the shrimp and cook until pink and cooked through, about 2-3 minutes per side.
7. Stir in the pineapple chunks and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes.
8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
9. Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves and serve hot.

**Storage & Serving:**

* Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
* Reheat the curry in the microwave or on the stovetop, adding a splash of water if necessary to prevent drying out.
* Serve with steamed rice or naan bread for a complete meal.

**Safety Notice:**
- Confirm allergen compliance: This recipe is free from nuts and tree nuts, making it suitable for individuals with nut allergies.
- Note cross-contamination risks: Ensure that all utensils and cutting boards used for preparing the shrimp are thoroughly cleaned and sanitized to prevent cross-contamination with other allergens.
- Recommend healthcare provider consultation for severe allergies: If you have a severe nut allergy, consult with your healthcare provider before consuming this recipe.

Recipe #2: Thai Coconut Curry with Shrimp and Vegetables

**Overview:**
Title: Khao Soi (Thai Coconut Curry with Shrimp and Vegetables)
Difficulty level: Medium
Time: 30 minutes (Prep: 15 minutes, Cook: 15 minutes, Total: 30 minutes)
Servings: 4
Comprehensive allergen and dietary compliance statement: This recipe is free from nuts, tree nuts, and common allergens. It is suitable for individuals with nut allergies and dietary restrictions.

**Ingredients:**

* 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
* 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
* 1 onion, diced
* 2 cloves garlic, minced
* 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
* 1 tablespoon Thai red curry paste
* 2 cups mixed vegetables (bell peppers, carrots, snap peas, and mushrooms)
* 1 can (14 oz) full-fat coconut milk
* 2 cups vegetable broth
* 1 tablespoon fish sauce (nut-free)
* 1 tablespoon lime juice
* 1 teaspoon palm sugar
* Salt and pepper, to taste
* Fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish
* Cooked jasmine rice, for serving

**Allergen markings:**

* Shrimp: contains fish (not a common allergen)
* Vegetable oil: may contain soy (check with manufacturer)
* Fish sauce: contains fish (not a common allergen)
* Coconut milk: may contain soy (check with manufacturer)

**Substitution options:**

* For shrimp: chicken breast or thighs, or tofu
* For mixed vegetables: adjust to your liking or use frozen vegetables
* For fish sauce: tamari or soy sauce (check for nut-free certification)

**Cross-contamination prevention notes:**

* Use separate cutting boards and utensils for raw shrimp and vegetables
* Wash hands thoroughly before handling ingredients
* Clean and sanitize all surfaces and equipment after use

**Instructions:**

1. Heat the oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
2. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger; cook, stirring occasionally, until the onion is translucent (3-4 minutes).
3. Add the curry paste; cook, stirring constantly, for 1 minute.
4. Add the mixed vegetables; cook, stirring occasionally, until they are tender-crisp (3-4 minutes).
5. Add the shrimp; cook, stirring occasionally, until they are pink and cooked through (2-3 minutes).
6. Stir in the coconut milk, vegetable broth, fish sauce, lime juice, and palm sugar. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
7. Reduce the heat to medium-low and cook, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened slightly (5-7 minutes).
8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
9. Garnish with cilantro leaves and serve with cooked jasmine rice.

**Storage & Serving:**

* Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
* Reheat the curry over low heat, stirring occasionally, until warmed through.
* Serve with cooked jasmine rice and garnish with cilantro leaves.

**Safety notice:**
This recipe is free from nuts and tree nuts, but please confirm with the manufacturer of the vegetable oil and coconut milk to ensure they do not contain soy or other common allergens. If you have a severe nut allergy, consult with your healthcare provider before consuming this recipe. Enjoy!

Title: Spicy Shrimp and Pineapple Curry
Difficulty level: Medium
Time: 30 minutes (Prep: 15 minutes, Cook: 15 minutes, Total: 30 minutes)
Servings: 4
Comprehensive allergen and dietary compliance statement: This recipe is free from nuts and tree nuts, making it suitable for individuals with nut allergies.

**Ingredients:**

* 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
* 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
* 2 cloves garlic, minced
* 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
* 2 tablespoons Thai red curry paste
* 2 cups coconut milk
* 1 cup pineapple chunks (fresh or canned)
* 1/4 cup fish sauce

Recipe #1: "Tuscan Chicken Shawarma"

**Overview:**
Title: Tuscan Chicken Shawarma
Difficulty level: Medium
Time: Prep 20 minutes, Cook 15 minutes, Total 35 minutes
Servings: 4
Comprehensive allergen and dietary compliance statement: This recipe is gluten-free, making it suitable for individuals with wheat allergies. However, please note that it contains dairy and soy. If you have a severe dairy or soy allergy, please consult a healthcare provider before consuming.

**Ingredients:**

* 1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into thin strips
* 1/4 cup olive oil
* 2 cloves garlic, minced
* 1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lemon juice
* 1 teaspoon dried oregano
* 1/2 teaspoon salt
* 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
* 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
* 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese (dairy)
* 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint
* 4 gluten-free pita breads (made from rice flour or corn flour)
* 1/4 cup tzatziki sauce (see below for recipe)
* 1/4 cup pickled red onion (see below for recipe)

**Tzatziki Sauce:**

* 1 cup plain Greek yogurt (dairy)
* 1/2 cup diced cucumber
* 1 tablespoon lemon juice
* 1/2 teaspoon salt
* 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
* 2 tablespoons chopped fresh dill

**Pickled Red Onion:**

* 1 large red onion, thinly sliced
* 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
* 1/4 cup water
* 1 tablespoon honey
* 1/4 teaspoon salt
* 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat a grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
2. In a large bowl, whisk together olive oil, garlic, lemon juice, oregano, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken and marinate for at least 15 minutes, or up to 30 minutes in the refrigerator.
3. Remove the chicken from the marinade and cook for 5-7 minutes per side, or until cooked through. Transfer to a plate and let rest for 5 minutes.
4. Meanwhile, toast the gluten-free pita breads by grilling them for 1-2 minutes per side, or baking them in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 5-7 minutes.
5. Assemble the shawarma by slicing the chicken into thin strips and placing it on the toasted pita breads. Top with tzatziki sauce, pickled red onion, parsley, and feta cheese.

**Storage & Serving:**

* Store leftover chicken in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven at 350°F (180°C) for 5-7 minutes, or until warmed through.
* Serve immediately, garnished with additional parsley and feta cheese if desired.

**Safety Notice:**
Please note that this recipe contains dairy and soy. If you have a severe dairy or soy allergy, please consult a healthcare provider before consuming. Additionally, ensure proper cross-contamination prevention when handling and storing ingredients. Enjoy your delicious and gluten-free Tuscan Chicken Shawarma!

Recipe #2: "Tuscan Chicken Cacciatore with Quinoa and Roasted Vegetables"

**Overview:**
Title: Tuscan Chicken Cacciatore with Quinoa and Roasted Vegetables
Difficulty level: Medium
Time: 45 minutes (Prep: 20 minutes, Cook: 20 minutes, Total: 45 minutes)
Servings: 4
Comprehensive allergen and dietary compliance statement: This recipe is gluten-free, making it suitable for individuals with wheat allergies. However, please note that it contains tree nuts (almonds) and may not be suitable for those with tree nut allergies. Consult a healthcare provider for severe allergies.

**Ingredients:**

* 1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into 1-inch pieces
* 2 tablespoons olive oil
* 1 large onion, chopped
* 2 cloves garlic, minced
* 1 cup mixed mushrooms (button, cremini, shiitake), sliced
* 1 cup roasted red bell pepper, diced
* 1 cup roasted zucchini, diced
* 1/2 cup quinoa
* 2 cups chicken broth
* 1 teaspoon dried oregano
* 1/2 teaspoon salt
* 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
* 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
* 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
* 2 tablespoons chopped almonds (for garnish)
* 2 tablespoons lemon juice (optional)

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
2. In a large bowl, whisk together quinoa and chicken broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes or until quinoa is tender and fluffy.
3. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Add chicken and cook until browned, about 5-7 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside.
4. In the same skillet, add chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add garlic and cook for an additional minute.
5. Add mushrooms, bell pepper, and zucchini to the skillet. Cook until vegetables are tender, about 5-7 minutes.
6. Add cooked chicken back into the skillet and stir to combine with vegetables.
7. Stir in oregano, salt, and pepper.
8. To assemble the dish, divide cooked quinoa among four plates. Top with chicken and vegetable mixture. Garnish with parsley, basil, and almonds. Serve with a squeeze of lemon juice, if desired.

**Storage & Serving:**

* Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
* Reheat in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for 10-15 minutes or until heated through.
* Serve immediately, garnished with additional parsley and basil if desired.

**Safety Notice:**
- Confirm gluten-free compliance
- Note tree nut presence (almonds) and potential cross-contamination risks
- Recommend healthcare provider consultation for severe tree nut allergies

Enjoy your delicious and gluten-free Tuscan Chicken Cacciatore with Quinoa and Roasted Vegetables! Buon appetito!

**Tahitian Vanilla Bean Panna Cotta with Coconut Cream and Fresh Mango**

**Overview:**
Title: Tahitian Vanilla Bean Panna Cotta with Coconut Cream and Fresh Mango
Difficulty level: Medium
Time: Prep (20 min), Cook (10 min), Total (30 min)
Servings: 4-6
Comprehensive allergen and dietary compliance statement: This recipe is free from milk and eggs, making it suitable for individuals with these allergies.

**Ingredients:**

* 1 can (14 oz) full-fat coconut milk
* 1/2 cup granulated sugar
* 1/4 cup unsweetened shredded coconut
* 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
* 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
* 1/2 vanilla bean, split lengthwise
* 1 cup fresh mango, diced
* 1/4 cup coconut cream, chilled
* Fresh mint leaves, for garnish

**Allergen markings:**

* Coconut milk (tree nut)
* Unsweetened shredded coconut (tree nut)

**Substitution options:**

* Replace coconut milk with a non-dairy milk alternative (e.g., almond or soy milk)
* Replace unsweetened shredded coconut with a different type of nut or seed (e.g., almonds or sunflower seeds)

3. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a large bowl, discarding the vanilla bean.
4. Pour the mixture into small individual serving cups or ramekins. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
5. Just before serving, top each panna cotta with a dollop of chilled coconut cream and a sprinkle of diced mango. Garnish with fresh mint leaves.

**Storage & Serving:**

* Store the panna cotta in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
* Serve chilled, garnished with fresh mint leaves and a sprinkle of unsweetened shredded coconut, if desired.
* Reheat the panna cotta by placing it in a saucepan with a little water over low heat, stirring occasionally, until warmed through.

**SAFETY NOTICE:**

Difficulty level: Medium
Time: 45 minutes (Prep: 20 minutes, Cook: 20 minutes, Total: 45 minutes)
Servings: 4
Comprehensive allergen and dietary compliance statement: This recipe is free from soy, gluten, and nuts. It is suitable for vegan and vegetarian diets.

**Ingredients:**

* 4 delicata squash (about 1 pound each)
* 1 tablespoon olive oil
* 1 small onion, diced
* 2 cloves garlic, minced
* 1 cup cooked black beans, rinsed and drained
* 1 cup cooked brown rice
* 1 teaspoon cumin
* 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
* 1/2 teaspoon salt
* 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
* 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
* 2 tablespoons lime juice
* 1/4 cup vegan sour cream (made from coconut milk or cashew cream)
* 1/4 cup diced fresh tomatoes
* 1/4 cup diced avocado
* 1/4 cup crumbled vegan queso fresco (made from cashew cream or nutritional yeast)

**Allergen markings:**

* Soy-free
* Gluten-free
* Nut-free

**Substitution options:**

* For black beans: cooked chickpeas or pinto beans
* For brown rice: cooked quinoa or cauliflower rice
* For vegan sour cream: plain Greek yogurt or coconut cream
* For vegan queso fresco: nutritional yeast or cashew cream

**Cross-contamination prevention notes:**

* Use separate cutting boards and utensils for handling the squash and filling ingredients to prevent cross-contamination.
* Wash your hands thoroughly before and after handling the squash and filling ingredients.

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
2. Cut the delicata squash in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds and pulp. Place the squash on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, cut side up.
3. Drizzle the olive oil over the squash and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Roast the squash in the preheated oven for 20 minutes, or until tender and caramelized.
4. While the squash is roasting, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
5. Add the cooked black beans, brown rice, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently, until the filling is heated through.
6. To assemble the stuffed squash, spoon the filling into the roasted squash halves, dividing it evenly among the four squash.
7. Top each squash half with a spoonful of vegan sour cream, diced tomatoes, diced avocado, and crumbled vegan queso fresco.
8. Garnish with chopped cilantro and a squeeze of lime juice.

**Storage & Serving:**

* Store the stuffed squash in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
* Reheat the stuffed squash in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for 10-15 minutes, or until warmed through.
* Serve the stuffed squash with a side of your favorite toppings, such as diced tomatoes, shredded lettuce, diced avocado, or sliced radishes.

**Safety notice:**

* Confirm that the vegan sour cream and vegan queso fresco are free from soy and other allergens.
* Note that cross-contamination with soy or other allergens is possible if the filling ingredients are not handled and stored properly.
* Recommend consulting with a healthcare provider for severe food allergies.

Recipe #1: "Tokyo-Style Chicken Gyoza with Spicy Miso Dipping Sauce"

**Overview:**
Title: Tokyo-Style Chicken Gyoza with Spicy Miso Dipping Sauce
Difficulty level: Medium
Time: Prep (20 min), Cook (15 min), Total (35 min)
Servings: 12-15 dumplings
Comprehensive allergen and dietary compliance statement: This recipe is free from fish, shellfish, nuts, and soy. It is suitable for vegetarians and vegans with gluten-free modifications.

**Ingredients:**

* 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast, finely chopped
* 1/2 cup finely chopped cabbage
* 1/4 cup finely chopped scallions (green onions)
* 2 cloves garlic, minced
* 1/4 cup gluten-free all-purpose flour
* 1/4 cup cornstarch
* 1/4 cup ice-cold water
* 1/4 teaspoon salt
* 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
* 1/4 teaspoon sesame oil
* 1/4 cup Spicy Miso Dipping Sauce (see below)
* Gluten-free dumpling wrappers (available at most Asian markets or online)

**Spicy Miso Dipping Sauce:**

* 1/2 cup white miso paste
* 1/4 cup rice vinegar
* 1/4 cup water
* 2 tablespoons Gochujang (Korean chili paste)
* 1 tablespoon honey
* 1/4 teaspoon grated ginger
* 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
* Salt and pepper to taste

**Instructions:**

1. In a large mixing bowl, combine chicken, cabbage, scallions, garlic, flour, cornstarch, salt, and pepper. Mix well until just combined.
2. Add ice-cold water and mix until a dough forms. Knead for 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
3. Divide dough into 12-15 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a ball and flatten slightly into a disk shape.
4. Place a tablespoon of the chicken mixture in the center of each disk. Fold the dough over the filling, forming a half-moon shape, and press edges together to seal.
5. Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add sesame oil and swirl to coat.
6. Add dumplings to the skillet, being careful not to overcrowd. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden brown.
7. Serve with Spicy Miso Dipping Sauce.

**Storage & Serving:**
Store cooked dumplings in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in a skillet with a little oil or in the microwave. Serve with Spicy Miso Dipping Sauce and garnish with additional scallions and sesame seeds if desired.

**Safety Notice:**
This recipe is free from fish, shellfish, nuts, and soy. However, please note that cross-contamination with gluten may occur if using gluten-containing ingredients. If you have a severe gluten allergy, please take necessary precautions to avoid exposure. Consult with a healthcare provider if you have any concerns. Enjoy!

Recipe #2: Japanese-Style Vegetable Gyoza with Miso-Glazed Carrots and Ginger-Soy Dipping Sauce

**Overview:**
Title: Gyoza no Niwa (Garden of Gyoza)
Difficulty level: Medium
Time: Prep (20 min), Cook (15 min), Total (35 min)
Servings: 4-6
Comprehensive allergen and dietary compliance statement: This recipe is free from fish and shellfish, gluten, and common allergens. It is suitable for vegetarians and vegans.

**Ingredients:**

* 1 package of gluten-free, vegan gyoza wrappers (or make your own using rice flour and water)
* 1 cup finely chopped cabbage
* 1 cup finely chopped carrots
* 1/2 cup finely chopped scallions
* 1/4 cup grated ginger
* 2 cloves garlic, minced
* 2 tablespoons soy sauce (make sure it's free from fish products)
* 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
* 1 tablespoon sesame oil
* 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
* Salt and pepper to taste
* Miso-Glazed Carrots (see below for recipe)
* Ginger-Soy Dipping Sauce (see below for recipe)

**Miso-Glazed Carrots:**

* 4-6 carrots, peeled and sliced into 1/4-inch thick rounds
* 2 tablespoons miso paste
* 2 tablespoons maple syrup
* 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
* 1 tablespoon sesame oil
* 1 teaspoon grated ginger
* Salt and pepper to taste

**Ginger-Soy Dipping Sauce:**

* 1/2 cup soy sauce (free from fish products)
* 1/4 cup rice vinegar
* 2 tablespoons grated ginger
* 1 tablespoon sesame oil
* 1 teaspoon honey
* Salt and pepper to taste

**Instructions:**

1. In a large mixing bowl, combine chopped cabbage, carrots, scallions, ginger, and garlic.
2. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, rice vinegar, and sesame oil. Pour the mixture over the vegetable mixture and stir until well combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
3. Lay a gyoza wrapper on a flat surface. Place a tablespoon of the vegetable mixture in the center of the wrapper. Fold the wrapper into a triangle by bringing the two opposite corners together to form a point. Press the edges together to seal the dumpling. Repeat with the remaining wrappers and filling.
4. Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add 1-2 tablespoons of sesame oil and swirl to coat the pan. When the oil is hot, add the dumplings to the pan, being careful not to overcrowd. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, until the dumplings are golden brown and crispy.
5. Serve the gyoza with Miso-Glazed Carrots and Ginger-Soy Dipping Sauce.

**Storage & Serving:**

* Store leftover gyoza in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in a pan with a little oil or in the microwave.
* Serve the gyoza with Miso-Glazed Carrots and Ginger-Soy Dipping Sauce. Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped scallions, if desired.

**Safety Notice:**
This recipe is free from fish and shellfish, gluten, and common allergens. However, please note that sesame is a common allergen and should be avoided if you have a sesame allergy. If you have a severe food allergy, please consult with a healthcare provider before consuming this recipe. Enjoy!
