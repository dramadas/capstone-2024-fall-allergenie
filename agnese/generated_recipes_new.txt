**Recipe 1: Thai Coconut Rice Bowl with Spicy Shrimp and Vegetables (No Nuts)**

**Allergen Information:**

* No tree nuts or peanuts are used in this recipe.
* Please note that this recipe is prepared in a facility that also handles gluten-containing ingredients. If you have a gluten allergy, please take necessary precautions to avoid cross-contamination.

**Cuisine Authenticity:**
This recipe is an authentic Thai-inspired dish, using traditional ingredients and techniques. The use of coconut milk and fish sauce gives it a rich and creamy flavor profile, while the spicy kick from the chilies adds depth and heat.

**Recipe Description:**
This recipe is a flavorful and nutritious Thai-inspired dish that combines succulent shrimp, crunchy vegetables, and creamy coconut rice in a spicy and aromatic curry sauce. The dish is served in a bowl, allowing you to customize with your favorite toppings and sauces.

**Key Details:**

* Difficulty: Medium
* Prep Time: 20 minutes
* Cook Time: 25 minutes
* Total Time: 45 minutes
* Servings: 4
* Dietary Notes:
	+ Allergen statement: This recipe is free from tree nuts and peanuts.
	+ Cross-contamination risks: This recipe is prepared in a facility that also handles gluten-containing ingredients. If you have a gluten allergy, please take necessary precautions to avoid cross-contamination.
	+ Religious/cultural compliance: This recipe is suitable for most dietary restrictions, including vegetarian and vegan options. However, please note that fish sauce is used, which may not be suitable for strict vegans.

**Ingredients:**

* 1 cup uncooked white rice
* 2 cups water
* 1 can (14 oz) full-fat coconut milk
* 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
* 2 cloves garlic, minced
* 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
* 1 tablespoon Thai red curry paste
* 1 teaspoon fish sauce (make sure it's free from nuts)
* 1/2 teaspoon salt
* 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
* 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
* 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, carrots, and snap peas)
* 2-3 Thai bird's eye chilies, sliced
* Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped (optional)
* Lime wedges, for serving
* Toasted sesame seeds, for garnish (make sure they are nut-free)

**Instructions:**

1. Cook the rice according to package instructions using 2 cups of water. Set aside.
2. In a large pan, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and ginger and cook for 1 minute, until fragrant.
3. Add the curry paste and cook for 1-2 minutes, until fragrant.
4. Add the coconut milk, fish sauce, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.
5. Add the shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes, until pink and cooked through.
6. Add the mixed vegetables and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until tender.
7. Stir in the sliced chilies and cook for an additional minute.
8. To assemble the bowls, place a scoop of cooked rice in the bottom, followed by a spoonful of the curry sauce and shrimp mixture. Garnish with chopped cilantro, toasted sesame seeds, and a lime wedge.

**Serving & Storage:**

* Serve immediately, or store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat before serving.
* To reheat, add a splash of water to the curry sauce and stir-fry the shrimp and vegetables in a pan until heated through.

**Final Safety Check:**

* This recipe is free from tree nuts and peanuts, but please note that it is prepared in a facility that also handles gluten-containing ingredients. If you have a gluten allergy, please take necessary precautions to avoid cross-contamination.

* 2 cloves garlic, minced

* 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as carrots, snap peas, and mushrooms)

**Recipe 1: Gluten-Free Italian Chicken Cacciatore with Zucchini Noodles (Zoodles)**

**Allergen Information:**

* Gluten-free
* No wheat, rye, barley, or triticale
* No cross-contamination with gluten-containing ingredients

**Cuisine Authenticity:**
This recipe is an Italian-inspired dish, specifically a Cacciatore-style chicken recipe, which is a traditional Italian hunter-style dish. The use of zucchini noodles (zoodles) instead of traditional pasta is a modern twist, making it a unique and gluten-free adaptation.

**Recipe Details:**

* Difficulty: Medium
* Prep Time: 20 minutes
* Cook Time: 25 minutes
* Total Time: 45 minutes
* Servings: 4
* Dietary Notes:
	+ Gluten-free
	+ No cross-contamination with gluten-containing ingredients
	+ Suitable for individuals with gluten intolerance or sensitivity

**Ingredients:**

* 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
* 2 medium zucchinis
* 2 tablespoons olive oil
* 1 onion, diced
* 2 cloves garlic, minced
* 1 cup mixed mushrooms (button, cremini, shiitake), sliced
* 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
* 1 teaspoon dried oregano
* 1 teaspoon dried basil
* Salt and pepper, to taste
* 1/4 cup gluten-free chicken broth
* 1/4 cup white wine (optional)
* Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

**Allergen Warnings:**

* None

**Cross-Contamination Prevention:**

* Use a dedicated gluten-free cooking space and utensils to prepare this recipe.
* Ensure all ingredients are gluten-free and stored separately from gluten-containing products.

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
2. Spiralize the zucchinis into noodles (zoodles) and set aside.
3. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned, about 5-6 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside.
4. In the same skillet, add the diced onion and cook until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
5. Add the sliced mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and start to brown, about 3-4 minutes.
6. Add the cherry tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally.
7. Add the gluten-free chicken broth and white wine (if using) to the skillet. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer.
8. Add the cooked chicken back to the skillet and stir to coat with the sauce.
9. Add the zoodles to the skillet and toss to combine with the chicken and sauce.
10. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the zoodles are tender.
11. Garnish with chopped parsley and serve hot.

**Serving & Storage:**

* Serve immediately, garnished with parsley.
* Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven or microwave until warmed through.

**Final Safety Check:**

* This recipe is gluten-free and designed to be safe for individuals with gluten intolerance or sensitivity.
* Always verify the ingredients and cooking space to ensure no cross-contamination with gluten-containing products.
* If you have a severe gluten allergy, consult with your healthcare provider before consuming this recipe.
* This recipe is an adaptation of a traditional Italian Cacciatore dish, using gluten-free ingredients and zucchini noodles to make it safe for those with gluten intolerance.

**Recipe #1: Coconut Lime Tapioca Pudding with Fresh Mango**

**Title & Origin:** Coconut Lime Tapioca Pudding with Fresh Mango (Thai-inspired dessert)

**Cultural Context:** In Thai cuisine, tapioca pudding is a popular dessert made with coconut milk, tapioca pearls, and various flavorings. This recipe is a creative twist on the traditional Thai dessert, substituting coconut milk with coconut cream and adding fresh mango for a sweet and tangy flavor combination.

**Allergen Compliance:** This recipe is free from milk, eggs, and other common allergens. However, please note that coconut is a common allergen, so please consult with your healthcare provider if you have a coconut allergy.

**Key Details:**

* Difficulty: Medium
* Prep Time: 20 minutes
* Cook Time: 30 minutes
* Total Time: 50 minutes
* Servings: 4-6
* Dietary Notes:
	+ Allergen statement: This recipe is free from milk, eggs, and other common allergens.
	+ Cross-contamination risks: None
	+ Religious/cultural compliance: This recipe is suitable for most dietary restrictions, including vegan and gluten-free diets.
	+ Additional dietary considerations: This recipe is high in sugar and calories, so it's best suited for special occasions or as an occasional treat.

**Ingredients:**

* 1 cup coconut cream (make sure it's free from milk and eggs)
* 1/2 cup tapioca pearls
* 1/4 cup granulated sugar
* 1/4 cup coconut water
* 1/2 teaspoon salt
* 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
* 1/4 teaspoon lime zest
* 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lime juice
* 1 ripe mango, diced
* Fresh mint leaves for garnish

**Allergen Warnings:**

* Coconut cream: If you're allergic to coconut, please substitute with a coconut-free cream alternative.
* Tapioca pearls: Make sure to choose tapioca pearls that are free from milk and eggs.

**Instructions:**

1. Rinse the tapioca pearls in cold water, then soak them in water for at least 4 hours or overnight. Drain and set aside.
2. In a medium saucepan, combine the coconut cream, sugar, coconut water, and salt. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar has dissolved and the mixture is hot but not boiling.
3. Add the vanilla extract, lime zest, and lime juice to the saucepan. Stir to combine.
4. Add the soaked tapioca pearls to the saucepan and cook, stirring constantly, for 5-7 minutes or until the pearls are cooked and the mixture has thickened.
5. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool slightly.
6. Stir in the diced mango and adjust the sweetness and lime juice to taste.
7. Pour the pudding into individual serving cups or a large serving dish. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
8. Garnish with fresh mint leaves and serve chilled.

**Serving & Storage:**

* Serve the pudding chilled, garnished with fresh mint leaves and a sprinkle of lime zest.
* Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat the pudding in the microwave or on the stovetop before serving.

**Final Safety Check:**

* This recipe is free from milk and eggs, but please consult with your healthcare provider if you have a coconut allergy.
* Always handle and store the pudding safely to prevent cross-contamination.
* If you experience any severe allergic reactions, please seek medical attention immediately.

**Allergen Compliance:** This recipe is free from milk, eggs, and common allergens. It's also gluten-free and suitable for those with nut allergies.

**Key Details:**

* Difficulty: Easy
* Prep Time: 20 minutes
* Cook Time: 30 minutes
* Total Time: 50 minutes
* Servings: 4-6
* Dietary Notes:
	+ Allergen statement: This recipe is free from milk, eggs, and common allergens.
	+ Cross-contamination risks: None
	+ Religious/cultural compliance: Suitable for most dietary restrictions
	+ Additional dietary considerations: Gluten-free, nut-free

**Ingredients:**

* 1 cup tapioca pearls
* 2 cups coconut water
* 1/2 cup coconut cream (make sure it's free from milk and eggs)
* 1/4 cup granulated sugar
* 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lime juice
* 1/4 teaspoon salt
* 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
* Fresh fruit of your choice (e.g., mango, pineapple, kiwi, or berries)

**Allergen Warnings:**

* Coconut cream: Make sure to choose a brand that's free from milk and eggs.
* Tapioca pearls: Some brands may contain gluten or other allergens. Choose a gluten-free and allergen-friendly option.

**Instructions:**

1. Rinse the tapioca pearls in cold water, then soak them in water for at least 4 hours or overnight. Drain and set aside.
2. In a medium saucepan, combine the coconut water, coconut cream, sugar, lime juice, and salt. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar dissolves and the mixture comes to a simmer.
3. Add the vanilla extract and tapioca pearls to the saucepan. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 20-25 minutes or until the tapioca pearls are cooked and the mixture has thickened.
4. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool slightly. Strain the pudding through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl to remove the tapioca pearls.
5. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate the pudding for at least 2 hours or overnight.
6. Just before serving, slice the fresh fruit and serve it on top of the chilled pudding.

**Serving & Storage:**
